Call for Entries

Bilingualism:
An Asset or a Liability?

The Big Question: How does living between two languages shape who you are?

Welcome to the 2027 J.U.C. Awards! This global competition empowers high school students (grades 9-12) and youth (aged 14-20) to move beyond being passive consumers and become Producers of Knowledge.

The Theme: The Linguistic Matrix

There is a Turkish proverb: "One language, one person; two languages, two people." This year, we want you to deeply analyze your lived bilingual experience. We are asking you to answer the central question: Is speaking two languages an advantage or a struggle in your daily life?

Is it an Asset?
(The Advantage) Does it act as a strategic tool, giving you intercultural confidence? Does it make you a "Living Bridge" connecting different cultures and ideas?
Is it a Liability?
(The Struggle) Or does it sometimes create a barrier? Does navigating dual linguistic lenses bring confusion, emotional weight, or a feeling of being caught between worlds?
Entry is completely free of charge.

Categories & Rules

Mandatory Rules for All Categories

  • ✔
    Originality: Must be original "Youth-Produced" work. AI-generated content is strictly prohibited.
  • ✔
    Native Language PDF: A copy of your submission in your native language must be included as a Word file (12 pt font) detailing the title, full name, school/organization, and country.
  • ✔
    Required Documents: You must submit a narrative bio (120–130 words in English) and a high-resolution portrait photo taken within the last 6 months.
  • ✔
    Consent Form: A signed Participation & Property Release is mandatory. If you are under 18, a parent or guardian must sign it.

Research & Writing Category

Analyze your lived bilingual experience to create new understanding and contribute to the global archive of human advancement.

Specific Requirements:

  • Word Count: Full word count must be exactly between 1,200 and 1,300 words.
  • Content: Must synthesize original insights—do not merely "search" for answers, but provide structured, intellectual identity outside standard school curricula.
  • Must adhere to all General Rules (Bio, Photo, Consent Form, Native Language PDF).

Poetry & Creative Expression

Use creative writing and poetry to express the emotional and cultural nuances of living between two languages.

Specific Requirements:

  • Focus on the theme "Bilingualism: An Asset or a Liability?" or the "Linguistic Matrix".
  • Must be completely original and AI-free.
  • Must adhere to all General Rules (Bio, Photo, Consent Form, Native Language PDF).

Media & Digital Arts

Leverage digital tools, art, and media to visually or interactively represent the "Living Bridge" of bilingualism.

Specific Requirements:

  • Submissions must clearly connect visual/digital media to the core theme of bilingualism.
  • Originality is strictly enforced.
  • Must include the required textual components: Narrative Bio, Consent Form, and Native Language documentation of the project concept.
