Call for Entries

Bilingualism:
An Asset or a Liability?

The Big Question: How does living between two languages shape who you are?

Welcome to the 2027 J.U.C. Awards. This global competition empowers high school students and youth to move beyond being passive consumers and become Producers of Knowledge.

The Theme: The Linguistic Matrix

There is a Turkish proverb: "One language, one person; two languages, two people." This year, we want you to deeply analyze your lived bilingual experience. We ask you to explore the central question: Is speaking two languages an advantage or a struggle in your daily life?

Is it an Asset?
(The Advantage) Does it act as a strategic tool, giving you intercultural confidence? Does it make you a "Living Bridge" connecting different cultures and ideas?
Is it a Liability?
(The Struggle) Or does it sometimes create a barrier? Does navigating dual linguistic lenses bring confusion, emotional weight, or a feeling of being caught between worlds?
Entry is completely free of charge.

Categories & Guidelines

Submission Categories: The Rigor of the Series

To maintain intellectual equity across all categories, we require a sustained creative effort. One single poem or one single image does not constitute a "Full Project." The following requirements ensure that the "Producer’s Capacity" is fully demonstrated.

Mandatory Rules for All Categories

  • I.
    Originality: Must be original "Youth-Produced" work. AI-generated content is strictly prohibited.
  • II.
    Native Language PDF: A copy of your submission in your native language must be included as a Word file (12 pt font) detailing the title, full name, school/organization, and country.
  • III.
    Required Documents: You must submit a narrative bio (120–130 words in English) and a high-resolution portrait photo taken within the last 6 months.
  • IV.
    Consent Form: A signed Participation & Property Release is mandatory. If you are under 18, a parent or guardian must sign it.

Research & Writing (The Weight of the Word)

Analyze your lived bilingual experience to create new understanding and contribute to the global archive of human advancement.

Specific Requirements:

  • Categories: Essays, Short Stories, Interviews, or Research Papers (Science/Tech/Sociology/Psychology).
  • Word Count: Full word count must be exactly between 1,200 and 1,300 words. (Plus bibliography for Research).
  • Content: Must synthesize original insights—do not merely "search" for answers, but provide structured, intellectual identity outside standard school curricula.
  • Must adhere to all General Rules (Bio, Photo, Consent Form, Native Language PDF).

Expanded Poetry & Creative Expression (The Rhythm of the Matrix)

Use creative writing to express the emotional and cultural nuances of living between two languages. We seek a "Literary Portfolio" rather than isolated thoughts.

The 3-5 Rule & Requirements:

  • Requirement: A collection of a minimum of 3 to 5 poems or a one-act play script. Single poems are not eligible.
  • Logic: Demonstrates consistency in style and thought. We want to see a sustained voice exploring the bilingual experience from multiple angles.
  • Thematic Thread: While each poem may stand alone, the collection should collectively explore the "Linguistic Matrix".
  • The Bridge: We encourage "Code-Switching" (using both languages) within the poems to illustrate the collision of cultures.

Media & Digital Arts (The Visual Narrative)

Leverage digital tools and media to visually represent the "Living Bridge" of bilingualism. This category requires significant editing and conceptual mapping to match the intellectual labor of the writing categories.

Specific Requirements:

  • Video: 3–7 minute video (MP4). Requires narration/subtitles and a "Complimentary Statement."
  • Still/Digital Art (The Series of 7): A series of exactly 7 high-resolution images (JPG/PNG/PDF). These images must function as a conceptual or narrative series, not random snapshots.
  • Note on Eligibility: Past cycles have seen a lack of eligible entries due to a failure to provide a connected series. To be eligible, you must include a Complimentary Statement (250–350 words) explaining the "unseen thread" that connects these 7 images or your video.
